Unveiling Photovoltaic Potential of Lead-free Cs3SbBiX9 Perovskites

Completed

First-principles screening of Cs3SbBiX9 (X = Cl, Br, I) identified Cs3SbBiI9 as a strong lead-free absorber, reaching 21.29% PCE in a champion device design.

01Overview

Lead-free halide perovskites are a key focus in green photovoltaics, but replacing Pb²⁺ with safer Sb³⁺/Bi³⁺ changes the crystal structure into vacancy-ordered double perovskites (A₃M₂X₉). This project screened the structural, electronic, optical, and mechanical properties of Cs3SbBiX9 (X = Cl, Br, I) — a heterovalent Sb/Bi-hybridized, halide-engineered alloy series — using first-principles DFT, then carried the most promising composition into SCAPS-1D device simulation.

Structures were optimized with the CASTEP module (Materials Studio) using Vanderbilt ultra-soft pseudopotentials and the GGA-PBE exchange-correlation functional, a 450 eV plane-wave cutoff, and a 4×4×3 Monkhorst-Pack k-point grid, with BFGS geometry optimization to tight convergence criteria.

02Key Findings — Material Screening
  • All three compounds crystallize in a trigonal (P321) vacancy-ordered double-perovskite structure; unit cell volume increases from Cl → Br → I as the larger iodide anion is incorporated.
  • Band structure calculations confirm all three are direct-bandgap semiconductors (VBM and CBM both at the Γ-point), with bandgaps narrowing from 2.763 eV (Cl) to 2.317 eV (Br) to 1.744 eV (I) — Cs3SbBiI9's bandgap sits in the ideal range for single-junction solar cells.
  • PDOS analysis shows the valence band is dominated by halogen p-orbitals and the conduction band by Sb/Bi p-orbitals, indicating strong, favorable covalent bonding for charge transport.
  • Cs3SbBiI9 showed the strongest visible-light absorption of the series (>2.5 × 10⁵ cm⁻¹) with the earliest absorption onset (~1.6 eV), while the chloride variant is near-transparent in the visible range and better suited to UV photodetection.
  • All three compositions satisfy the Born stability criteria for trigonal crystals, confirming mechanical stability; stiffness (bulk and Young's modulus) increases with heavier halides, while overall low elastic moduli suggest flexibility favorable for flexible optoelectronics.
03Device-Level Optimization (SCAPS-1D)
  • Using Cs3SbBiI9 as the absorber, electron transport layer (ETL) candidates were screened with Cu2O fixed as the HTL — WS2 emerged as the champion ETL, reaching 21.28% efficiency at a 900 nm absorber thickness.
  • With WS2 fixed as the ETL, hole transport layer (HTL) candidates were then screened — Cu2O remained the strongest performer, reaching 21.31% efficiency at the same thickness.
  • The finalized ITO/WS2/Cs3SbBiI9/Cu2O/Au device achieved Voc = 1.264 V, Jsc = 20.012 mA/cm², FF = 83.09%, and PCE = 21.29% at 900 nm and 300 K — competitive against other reported lead-free perovskite designs.
  • A temperature sweep from 280 K to 400 K showed efficiency dropping from 21.94% to 16.57%, driven mainly by falling Voc and fill factor, indicating strong low-temperature performance but sensitivity to heat.
