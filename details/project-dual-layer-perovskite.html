Design and Simulation of an All-Inorganic CZTSSe/ZnSnN2 Dual-Absorber Solar Cell

Completed

01Overview

Single-junction CZTSSe solar cells are limited by an open-circuit voltage deficit caused by intrinsic defects and recombination. This project explores a dual-absorber architecture to get around that limit: a narrow-bandgap CZTSSe top absorber (1.142 eV) is paired with a wide-bandgap ZnSnN2 bottom absorber (1.50 eV) in the stack FTO/ZnO/CZTSSe/ZnSnN2/NiOx/Au.

ZnSnN2 harvests high-energy photons to boost voltage, while CZTSSe captures the lower end of the spectrum — together reducing recombination losses and outperforming single-junction kesterite designs. Both materials are non-toxic and earth-abundant, and the all-inorganic stack is expected to offer better long-term stability than organic hole-transport materials.

The cell was modeled in SCAPS-1D under standard AM1.5G illumination (1000 W/m², 300 K), solving Poisson's equation and the electron/hole continuity and drift-diffusion equations across the stack. Gold (work function 5.47 eV) was selected as the back contact after simulations showed a work function above 5.4 eV was required at that interface.

02Optimization & Results
  • Acceptor doping of the CZTSSe layer was swept from 1015–1018 cm-3; Voc rose from 0.89 V toward 0.95 V with increasing NA, with peak efficiency near NA = 1017 cm-3. ZnSnN2 doping had comparatively little effect on performance.
  • Absorber thickness was then optimized: a 1.2 µm CZTSSe layer gave the best performance, while ZnSnN2 thickness proved far less sensitive — 0.8 µm was chosen to balance high-energy photon capture against material use.
  • ETL (ZnO) and HTL (NiOx) thicknesses were optimized last, each swept from 0.02–0.2 µm; both layers were kept thin (0.05 µm) in the final design.
  • Final optimized performance: Voc = 0.8915 V, Jsc = 42.18 mA/cm², FF = 83.75%, PCE = 31.52% — ahead of comparable CZTS/CZTSSe/ZnSnN2-based designs reported in the literature.
03Possible Fabrication Route

A proposed fabrication path was also outlined: a dense 50 nm ZnO ETL deposited on FTO via ALD or sputtering, a 1.2 µm CZTSSe absorber formed by sputtering metallic precursors followed by selenization at 500–600°C, a 0.8 µm ZnSnN2 layer via reactive RF magnetron sputtering, a 50 nm NiOx HTL by spin-coating or sputtering, and a 100 nm thermally evaporated gold contact — with interface passivation needed between the two absorber layers to control defect density.
